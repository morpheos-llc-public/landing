About Me

Brandon Hogstad, Ph.D., is a principal consultant and business development strategist with extensive experience in scientific due diligence, commercialization, partnerships, and alliance management. As the founder of Morpheos Innovation Services LLC, he works with startups, investors, and academic institutions—across both biotechnology and AI-driven platforms—to bring breakthrough innovations to market.

Brandon’s portfolio includes building re-branding strategies for preclinical pharmacology CROs, guiding IP strategies and out-licensing activities for cutting-edge technologies, and driving strategic partnering initiatives for AI-driven data analysis and therapeutic discovery platforms.

Prior to founding Morpheos, Brandon served as Associate Director of the Colton Center for Autoimmunity and as Business Development Manager for the Office of Therapeutics Alliances at NYU Langone Health. In these roles, he managed a $1M annual project pipeline aimed at novel autoimmune therapeutics and diagnostics, collaborating with industry giants such as Sanofi. Among his achievements, Brandon spearheaded key research collaborations—including a pivotal partnership with Pfizer—that contributed to the development of nirmatrelvir, the antiviral component of Paxlovid.

Following his tenure at NYU, Brandon became Associate Director of Scientific Business Development at Immunai, where he leveraged AI-powered technologies to establish strategic partnerships and advance the company’s mission of harnessing single-cell genomics for precision immunotherapies. His broad expertise spans target validation, preclinical study design, competitive landscape analysis, and AI-driven platform strategy, regularly engaging with key opinion leaders to refine commercialization roadmaps.

Brandon earned his Ph.D. in Biomedical Sciences from the Icahn School of Medicine at Mount Sinai, with scientific contributions published in journals such as Nature and Journal of Experimental Medicine. His work reflects an unwavering commitment to advancing medical science, improving global health, and fostering innovation at the intersection of biotechnology and artificial intelligence.
